Children with intellectual and developmental disabilities (IDD) are individuals who have difficulties with cognitive and intellectual functioning. These difficulties can impact their daily activities, including learning, communication, and personal care. IDD can be present from birth or may develop later in life as a result of an injury or illness.
Some common difficulties associated with IDD

1...Difficulty with language and communication: Children with IDD may have difficulty with speaking, understanding spoken language, and using gestures or signs to communicate
2...Delays in motor development: Children with IDD may have difficulty with gross motor skills (such as walking and running) or fine motor skills (such as writing or using utensils).
3...Learning disabilities: Children with IDD may have difficulties with learning and retaining new information, and may require specialized educational support.
4...Social and emotional challenges: Children with IDD may struggle with socializing and building relationships, and may have behavioral and emotional challenges.
5...Adaptive skills: Children with IDD may have difficulty with everyday tasks such as self-care, dressing, and personal hygiene.
It's crucial to remember that every kid with IDD has different issues, and both the degree and nature of those challenges can vary substantially. Many kids with IDD may live happy, meaningful lives with the correct care and treatment.

THE Therapy WE Offer

In general, it's critical to collaborate closely with a medical expert and other specialists to create a treatment plan that caters to the child's particular requirements and enables them to realise their full potential.

Occupational Therapy

A well-liked treatment choice is occupational therapy, which may help children with IDDs acquire the abilities they need for daily life, such as dressing, bathing, and eating.

Speech and language therapy

Children who struggle with communication may benefit from speech and language therapy, which can enhance their language abilities and improve their capacity for self-expression.

Physical therapy

For kids with physical disabilities or mobility concerns, physical therapy might be beneficial. However, drugs must always be taken in combination with other treatments and under a doctor's close observation.

Cognitive behavioral therapy

Children with IDDs may also benefit from psychotherapy, such as cognitive behavioural therapy (CBT), as they may have trouble interacting socially and might use some coping mechanisms.

OUR PRIMERYConcern

Providing children with intellectual and developmental impairments with the resources and assistance they need to realise their full potential is the main priority. This covers having access to educational and therapeutic resources, as well as opportunities for community integration and deep connections. Promoting inclusion and removing obstacles to their social involvement are critical..

OUR Mission AND Vision

Our goal in treating children with IDDs is to help them realise their full potential and live happy, meaningful lives while also fostering inclusion and acceptance in society.This is the core of our purpose and vision.

MISSION

To provide high-quality, evidence-based therapy and support services to children with IDDs and their families.

To help children with IDDs reach their full potential and lead fulfilling and independent lives.

To promote inclusivity and acceptance of children with IDDs in society.

To advocate for the rights and needs of children with IDDs and their families.

VISION

A world in which children with IDDs have equal opportunities to participate in all aspects of life, including education, employment, and social activities.

A world in which children with IDDs are supported and empowered to reach their full potential and lead fulfilling lives.

A world in which families of children with IDDs have access to the resources and support they need to care for their children and promote their development.

OUR Team OF Experts

Our team of professionals offers tailored and comprehensive care to children with intellectual and developmental difficulties. This group often consists of educators, healthcare professionals, and therapists who work together to meet the particular requirements of each child and enable them to realise their full potential. For these kids and their families, the objective is to foster participation, independence, and a good quality of life.
