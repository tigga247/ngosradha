Children with intellectual and developmental disabilities (IDD) are individuals who have difficulties with cognitive and intellectual functioning. These difficulties can impact their daily activities, including learning, communication, and personal care. IDD can be present from birth or may develop later in life as a result of an injury or illness.
Some common difficulties associated with IDD

1...Difficulty with language and communication: Children with IDD may have difficulty with speaking, understanding spoken language, and using gestures or signs to communicate
2...Delays in motor development: Children with IDD may have difficulty with gross motor skills (such as walking and running) or fine motor skills (such as writing or using utensils).
3...Learning disabilities: Children with IDD may have difficulties with learning and retaining new information, and may require specialized educational support.
4...Social and emotional challenges: Children with IDD may struggle with socializing and building relationships, and may have behavioral and emotional challenges.
5...Adaptive skills: Children with IDD may have difficulty with everyday tasks such as self-care, dressing, and personal hygiene.
It's crucial to remember that every kid with IDD has different issues, and both the degree and nature of those challenges can vary substantially. Many kids with IDD may live happy, meaningful lives with the correct care and treatment.

OUR PRIMARY Issue

Children with intellectual and developmental disabilities (IDD) may have a range of challenges and issues, such as trouble communicating, interacting with others, and displaying adaptive behaviour. They could also struggle with fine and gross motor abilities, as well as scholastic activities and learning. Additionally, emotional and behavioural issues including anxiety, despair, and anger can affect children with IDD. Additionally, it may be difficult for kids with IDD to get the right kind of schooling, healthcare, and other assistance. To support children with IDD and help them reach their full potential, families, carers, and professionals must collaborate. Individualized education plans, counselling, and other treatments, along with promoting the use of the right resources, may all be part of this.

Communication difficulties

Children with IDD have difficulty with verbal and nonverbal communication, including expressive language, receptive language, and social communication.

Social interaction difficulties

Children with IDD may have difficulty with social interactions, including understanding social cues, initiating interactions, and maintaining relationships.

Adaptive behavior difficulties

Children with IDD may have difficulty with daily living skills, including self-care, problem-solving, and independent living.

Academic difficulties

Children with IDD may have difficulty with academic tasks, including reading, writing, and math.

Motor skill difficulties

Children with IDD may have difficulty with fine and gross motor skills, including coordination, balance, and dexterity.

Emotional and behavioral difficulties

Children with IDD may experience emotional and behavioral problems, such as anxiety, depression, and aggression.

EVENTS WE Cunduct

Our NGO conducts a variety of activities and events to support the growth and well-being of children with intellectual and developmental impairments. These activities are designed to improve their abilities and provide them opportunity to grow and live fulfilled lives.

Therapeutic interventions.

such as speech therapy, occupational therapy, and physical therapy to help children develop skills and improve their overall functioning.

Educational programs

such as special education classes and tutoring to help children with intellectual and developmental disabilities learn and succeed in school.

Vocational training.

to teach children with intellectual and developmental disabilities the skills they need to find and maintain employment.

Social skills groups

to help children with intellectual and developmental disabilities develop the social skills they need to interact with others and build friendships.

Support groups

for children with intellectual and developmental disabilities and their families to provide emotional support and resources for coping with the challenges of living with a disability.

Recreational activities

such as sports, music, and art programs to provide children with intellectual and developmental disabilities with opportunities for physical activity and creative expression.
